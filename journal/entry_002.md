## The Metric Tensor Is the General Pythagorean Theorem

In flat space:
$$ds^2 = dx^2 + dy^2$$

That's $c^2 = a^2 + b^2$. The metric tensor is the identity matrix. No curvature. No tax.

In curved space:
$$ds^2 = g_{ij} \, dx^i \, dx^j$$

The off-diagonal terms and curvature corrections give you $\Delta$.

My manifold already computes $c^2$. I just didn't call it that.
